DiaMom Mobile App — Validation Report

docs/PRD.md
Fair

The DiaMom PRD has a clear product thesis: a Bahasa Indonesia pregnancy support companion centered on education, breathing, Labor Dance guidance, VAS tracking, and safety-first session flow. It is strong as a product framing draft, but only fair as a build handoff because health/safety policy, MVP boundaries, privacy obligations, and measurable acceptance criteria remain too underspecified for downstream UX, architecture, and story generation.

The biggest risk is not missing screens; the document already lists many screens. The risk is that the PRD repeatedly says "safe", "proper privacy", "reviewed by a qualified healthcare professional", and "blocked or warned" without converting those phrases into explicit go/no-go rules, required copy, data handling constraints, and testable acceptance criteria.

Decision-readiness

thin

The product direction is clear, but the highest-impact safety decisions are blurred. In particular, "blocked or warned" is not a decision for a pregnancy movement app.

high

Safety gating policy is ambiguous

§11 FR-003
Risky answers do not map to exact user outcomes.
Fix: Add a safety decision table with severity, copy, allowed next actions, and education-only access rules.
high

Exercise warning signs are incomplete

§8.2, §8.10
The checklist omits several common pregnancy exercise stop signs such as chest pain, headache, calf pain/swelling, contractions, and leaking fluid.
Fix: Align screening and warnings with clinically reviewed guidance before release.

Substance over theater

adequate

The screen inventory, VAS flow, history, and emergency flow are earned by the product thesis. The NFRs are the main weak spot because they remain broad rather than testable.

medium

Non-functional requirements are too generic

§12
"Feel fast", "smoothly", and "proper consent" need measurable thresholds.
Fix: Add target launch time, tap target size, contrast, screen reader coverage, storage, analytics, and crash-log rules.

Strategic coherence

adequate

The safety plus guided practice plus VAS history loop is coherent. The MVP, however, is broad enough that the team needs a vertical-slice strategy.

high

MVP is oversized without a slice strategy

§16
The Must Have list spans many workstreams before the core safety loop is proven.
Fix: Define the smallest approved vertical slice for onboarding, one session, VAS, history, and emergency stop.

Done-ness clarity

thin

FR IDs and acceptance criteria exist, but critical flows do not yet define the exact states, transitions, validation rules, or release blockers needed for implementation.

high

Safety-critical FRs lack testable state machines

FR-002, FR-003, FR-012
Onboarding, screening, and emergency stop behavior can be implemented in multiple incompatible ways.
Fix: Add state tables for consent, screening, pre-session checks, interrupted sessions, and emergency stop.
medium

VAS handling is underspecified

FR-010, FR-013, FR-014
Thresholds, high-score escalation, incomplete result storage, and result wording are not defined.
Fix: Define VAS thresholds, escalation copy, chart rules, and anti-overclaiming wording.

Scope honesty

thin

Should Have and Could Have lists help, but the PRD needs explicit non-goals and review triggers for health, privacy, backend, clinician, and AI features.

high

No explicit regulatory/product boundary

§3, §14, §18
The app says it is not diagnosis or treatment, but prohibited recommendations and future review triggers are not defined.
Fix: Add Regulatory Boundary and Non-Goals sections.
high

Privacy scope is under-specified

§12, §14
Sensitive reproductive health data requirements need more than local storage and deletion.
Fix: Add consent, retention, third-party sharing, analytics SDK, privacy notice, deletion, and legal review requirements.

Downstream usability

thin

The PRD has useful raw material, but screens, FRs, data models, analytics events, edge cases, and metrics are not mapped together.

medium

Missing traceability across screens, FRs, data, events, and metrics

§9-§18
Story generation will have to infer ownership and test coverage.
Fix: Add a compact traceability table.
medium

No user journeys with named personas

§5, §10
Target users and flows exist, but not end-to-end journey requirements.
Fix: Add journeys for first-time mother, returning quick session, high-risk stop, and midwife-assisted education.

Shape fit

thin

This is currently shaped like a screen inventory plus feature list. For a pregnancy movement product, it needs stronger safety, privacy, content governance, and open-question structure before epics and stories.

high

Under-formalized for a pregnancy movement product

§6, §8, §11, §12, §19
Safety and privacy are acknowledged but not converted into release-blocking requirements.
Fix: Add Safety Policy, Privacy/Data Handling, Regulatory Boundary, Content Governance, and Open Questions sections.

Mechanical notes

  • FR IDs are contiguous from FR-001 to FR-017.
  • No Open Questions, [ASSUMPTION], or [NOTE FOR PM] callouts are present.
  • User-facing labels mix Bahasa Indonesia and English despite Bahasa Indonesia being the primary language.
  • No glossary or traceability table is present.
